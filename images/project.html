Home Lab Journey

I am a tech enthusiast and I love to learn new things revolving the tech field.

Over the the course of my last few months i have been working on various projects my more proud one is my home lab that I have built from scratch.

It all started when began simply bypassing the accessibility of my campus network, I used a router with MAC address cloning establishing a private subnet in my dormitory this allowed me to have full control of my network traffic as well as route traffic

I took a course last semester called Intro to OS and Networks, throughout this course I learn about the virtualization platform called VirtualBox.This course provided the crucial foundation and understanding of Type-2 hypervisors, the logic of virtual switching, and the nuances of hardware abstraction. virtual cyberlab to test and learn about various networking concepts like configuring a virtual firewall and routing VLANs to different virtual machines like kali linux Metasploite and Ubuntu server.

Running this entire stack on my primary desktop meant interference with daily use of my computer this what when I needed dedicated compute resources.That experience sparked my interest in building my own physical home lab so that I could have a dedicated environment for testing and learning without interfering with my daily computing needs running 24/7.

I started of by purchasing two micro-PCs and Dell Optiplex Mini and an HP helpdesk 800 G1 installed Proxmox VE allowing me to configure the node as a cluster centralized management.I experimented with the 2 nodes for a bit where I was learning network architecture and segmentation with VLAN configuration and mainly experimenting with variety of services, alongside running labs like penetration testing and vulnerability assessment.

That wasnt enough for me, I eventually expanded to a three-node setup establishing quorum using a Tplink managed switch to segment my traffic from router directly to nodes. I was then able to get my hands on a Dell Optiplex 980 on campus where I installed Opensense transforming it into a physical firewall and router From there I was able to move my node from my home network to a dedicated management VLAN through 802.1 VLAN tagging, ensuring that management traffic and lab traffic are logically isolated. I was able to get my hands on a Raspberry Pi 3B+ where I placed on a dedicated VLAN. Configured AdGuard as high level DNS filtering and Unbound as a recursive DNS resolver, eliminating reliance on third-party upstream providers like Google or Cloudflare for better privacy and security.
